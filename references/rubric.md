# Ad Audit Rubric (DTC video ads — Meta & TikTok)

This is the scoring framework. Each criterion has a weight; the weights sum to
100. Score each one on its own sub-scale, then sum for the overall score.

The point of weighting is to reflect what actually moves performance on paid
social. A beautiful ad with a weak hook loses to an ugly ad with a great one —
so the hook is worth far more than production polish. Keep that hierarchy in
mind when a video forces a trade-off.

Ground every score in something you can actually see in a frame or read in the
transcript. If you can't assess a criterion (e.g. no audio, so no voiceover),
say "not assessable" and redistribute its weight proportionally across the rest
rather than guessing — don't penalize a video for something you couldn't judge.

---

## Criteria & weights

### 1. Hook — first 3 seconds (22 pts)
The most important thing in the whole ad. In the feed the viewer decides to keep
watching or scroll within ~1–2 seconds, so the opening has to earn attention
immediately. Judge it from the first ~6 frames (t0.0–t3.0) and the first line of
the transcript together.

- **Great (18–22):** Instant pattern interrupt — motion, a bold visual, a
  surprising statement, a relatable problem, or the product already in action.
  The viewer knows within 3s what this is about and why they'd care.
- **OK (11–17):** Watchable but slow to commit — a few seconds before the point
  lands, or a generic opener that doesn't differentiate.
- **Weak (0–10):** Slow logo/brand intro, a black screen or title card, a long
  establishing shot, or nothing that creates curiosity or tension. This is the
  most common reason ads fail.

### 2. Message & value-proposition clarity (14 pts)
By the end, is it unmistakable what's being sold, who it's for, and why it's
better than the alternative? Look for a clear problem → solution → benefit arc.

- **Great:** One sharp core message; benefits (not just features) are obvious;
  no confusion about the product.
- **Weak:** Vague, tries to say five things, or you finish unsure what the
  product even is or does.

### 3. Pacing & retention (12 pts)
Momentum that keeps someone watching. Judge from how much the frames change over
the timeline plus the overall length.

- **Great:** Visual variety, scene/angle changes every few seconds, no dead air;
  length fits the platform (15–30s is the sweet spot for most DTC).
- **Weak:** Long static holds, repetitive shots, a draggy middle, or padded
  length with nothing happening.

### 4. Call to action (10 pts)
A clear, specific next step — ideally spoken AND on-screen, near the end.

- **Great:** Explicit ask ("Tap the link / Shop now / Get yours today"), often
  paired with an offer or light urgency, on a clean end card.
- **Weak:** No CTA, a vague one, or it flashes by too fast to register.

### 5. Script / voiceover quality (10 pts)
From the transcript. Is the writing conversational, tight, and persuasive?

- **Great:** Strong first line, talks like a person (not a brochure), one idea
  per sentence, benefit-led, lands a natural CTA. No rambling.
- **Weak:** Jargon, corporate tone, buries the point, rambles, or the audio is
  garbled / mostly empty when speech was expected.
- **Not assessable:** No audio track, or transcription unavailable — skip and
  redistribute the weight; note that the script wasn't reviewed.

### 6. Captions & on-screen text (9 pts)
A large share of feed views are watched on mute, so the ad must work silently.
Check the frames for burned-in captions/text.

- **Great:** Readable captions or key-message text on screen, well-sized, synced,
  inside the safe zone. The ad makes sense with the sound off.
- **Weak:** No text at all (relies entirely on audio), tiny/low-contrast text, or
  text buried under platform UI.

### 7. Social proof & authenticity (8 pts)
Trust signals and a native, non-"ad" feel.

- **Great:** Testimonials, reviews, real-person UGC, demonstrations, before/after,
  user counts — something that builds credibility. Feels organic to the platform.
- **Weak:** Polished corporate spot with zero proof, or an obviously staged feel
  that screams "advertisement" on a UGC-first platform like TikTok.

### 8. Production quality — visual + audio (8 pts)
Watchability. UGC and phone-shot footage is totally fine (often better) — the bar
is "clear and intentional," not "expensive."

- **Great:** In focus, well-lit, stable enough, framed on the subject; audio is
  clean and audible.
- **Weak:** Pixelated/blurry, dark, shaky to the point of distraction, or muddy/
  clipping audio.

### 9. Mobile fit & safe zones (5 pts)
Right shape for the placement, and important content kept clear of platform UI.
Use the detected aspect ratio + references/platform-specs.md.

- **Great:** Vertical 9:16 (or 4:5) for Reels/TikTok; text and CTA sit inside the
  safe zone, clear of the TikTok right-rail icons and the top/bottom UI bands.
- **Weak:** Letterboxed landscape crammed into a vertical slot, or key text/CTA
  hidden behind the caption area, profile icons, or the CTA button.

### 10. Branding presence (2 pts)
Product/brand shown early and recurrently — not saved for a logo at the very end.
Intentionally low weight: under-branding costs a little, but a slow branded
intro is a hook problem (penalize it there), and over-branding hurts more than
it helps on paid social.

## Verdict bands

Map the total score to a verdict:

- **🟢 LAUNCH-READY — 80–100:** Strong creative. Ship it / scale it. List only
  minor polish.
- **🟡 NEEDS WORK — 60–79:** Real potential, fixable problems. Give a ranked
  fix list; the hook and clarity issues come first.
- **🔴 REWORK — below 60:** Fundamental issues that will likely cap performance.
  Be direct about what to rebuild, starting with the hook.

## Scoring discipline
- Be specific and cite timestamps ("at 0:00 you open on a 2-second logo
  animation"). Vague feedback isn't actionable.
- Don't inflate. An honest 64 the user can act on beats a polite 82.
- Separate "bad" from "not shown" — if audio is missing, the script is *not
  assessable*, not a zero.
